Comment 2: How do the hyper-parameters, e.g. of the CRP, effect the performance?

Feedback: The effects of hyper-parameters are usually complex in most models.
α\alphaα: the learning rate of stochastic gradient descent (SGD). Its effect is a common issue of iteration methods. Small learning rates make the loss function slowly converge, while large rates make the loss function randomly oscillate.
β\betaβ: the hyper-parameter of CRP. It controls the number of semantic vectors of each entity. Larger β\betaβ means more semantic vectors. Larger or smaller number of semantic vectors will decrease the performance. And too large number (too big CRP factor) also results in increasing training time.
γ\gammaγ: It controls the number of triples which need to be trained. In our model, if the probability of a triple in training dataset is eγe^{\gamma}e​γ​​ times larger than the probability of its negative sample, this triple needs not to be trained.

Comment 3: How sensitive is the approach as the completeness/reliability of the type information changes?

Feedback: We also note this issue. In order to mitigate their effects, we use a special intersection (eq4) of entity type sets to construct relation types. Because relation types related to types of many entities, the impact of completeness/reliability of types can be decreased. And we also complement entity-type sets with these more reliable relation types.
It is also a good idea to consider the robustness of models.

Feedback: CRP determines if an entity needs a new vector in training process. The key of CRP is the generation probability which is Pnew,tail(h,r,t)P_{new,tail}(h,r,t)P​new,tail​​(h,r,t) (eq10) in the paper. Each run will generate different numbers of vectors. However, the difference of performances is small. We apply their averages for performance evaluations. This is a feature of probabilistic models.

Feedback: Original triples mean the triples in testing datasets (before replace). The “filter” settings mean removing these triples already existing in datasets from the prediction results. The filter measure is widely used in previous works.
